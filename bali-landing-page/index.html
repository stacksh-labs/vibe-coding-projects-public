A World Apart

Nestled among thousands of Indonesian islands, Bali stands alone — a jewel of emerald rice terraces, sacred mountains and sun-drenched shores. It is a place where the spiritual and the sensual exist side by side, where every sunrise is greeted with offerings of flowers and incense, and every dusk is painted in shades of fire.

The Balinese call their home Pulau Dewata, the Island of Gods. Spend even a single day here and you will understand why.

01 — Culture

Living Tradition

🌝

Ngaben — The Fire Ceremony

Cremation in Bali is not a time of grief but of joyful release. Elaborate towers and effigies are set alight during the Ngaben ceremony, freeing the soul to ascend and be reincarnated. Strangers are welcome to watch — and to marvel.

🎠

Kecak — The Monkey Dance

At sunset in Uluwatu, a chorus of bare-chested men sways and chants in hypnotic unison, enacting scenes from the Ramayana epic. The rhythm rises, the flames leap, and for a moment time itself seems to stop.

🌸

Daily Offerings

Every morning, Balinese women craft canang sari — small palm-leaf trays woven with flowers, rice and incense — and place them at doorways, shrines and street corners as a quiet, daily act of gratitude to the gods.

02 — Landscapes

Nature's Masterpiece

Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Sculpted over centuries, these stepped emerald fields above Ubud are a testament to the Balinese subak irrigation system — a UNESCO-protected philosophy of water, community and devotion.

Mount Agung

The sacred volcano towers over the island at 3,142 m, its summit often veiled in cloud. For the Balinese it is the navel of the world.

Nusa Dua & Seminyak

White sand, turquoise water and the legendary Balinese sunset — the beaches here feel like the edge of the world.

03 — Temples

Sacred Stones

With over 20,000 temples, Bali is a living sanctuary. Ancient pura rise from cliff edges, jungle clearings and ocean rocks, draped in black-and-white poleng cloth and perfumed by frangipani blossoms.

01

Tanah Lot

Perched on a sea stack battered by the Indian Ocean, Tanah Lot is Bali's most photographed temple — especially at sunset when the sky turns molten gold behind its silhouette.

02

Pura Besakih

Known as the Mother Temple of Bali, Besakih clings to the slopes of Mount Agung at 1,000 metres. Its complex of 23 separate temples makes it the largest and holiest site on the island.

03

Uluwatu Temple

Standing 70 metres above the crashing waves of the Bukit Peninsula, Uluwatu is guarded by a tribe of mischievous macaques and framed by the most spectacular ocean views in all of Bali.

04

Tirta Empul

A spring-fed water temple near Tampaksiring, Tirta Empul's crystal pools have been used for ritual purification since 962 AD. Pilgrims submerge themselves beneath holy spouts to cleanse body and spirit alike.

04 — Cuisine

A Feast for the Senses

Balinese cuisine is an aromatic symphony of galangal, lemongrass, turmeric and chilli — food that nourishes not just the body but the soul.

🍖

Babi Guling

Whole suckling pig slow-roasted over coconut husks and stuffed with a rich spice paste of turmeric, ginger and kaffir lime. A ceremonial staple turned beloved street food.

🍲

Nasi Goreng

Indonesia's iconic fried rice, elevated in Bali with a glossy fried egg, crisp shallots, prawn crackers and a secret sambal that lingers on the palate long after the last bite.

🥗

Lawar

A vibrant mix of finely chopped vegetables, grated coconut and minced meat bound together with the rich spice base base genep. Every village, every family, has its own cherished recipe.

☕

Kopi Bali

Balinese coffee — thick, bold and brewed with grounds left to settle — is best sipped slowly at a hillside warung as the mist rolls over the jungle canopy below.
